Statistical Analysis of Local General Practice Patients

In modern healthcare, statistical analysis plays a vital role in unraveling complex relationships between various health factors and their impact on patient well-being. Understanding the interplay between different variables can provide invaluable insights into disease patterns, risk factors, and general health trends. This statistical analysis aims to explore the relationship between several key factors, including Body Mass Index (BMI), age, systolic blood pressure, disease status, and their associations with general health trends among patients at a local General Practice (GP).


The General Practice setting serves as a primary point of contact for individuals seeking medical care, making it an ideal environment to conduct comprehensive health assessments and gather relevant data. By analyzing the data collected from patients, we can uncover patterns and associations that may inform medical professionals and policymakers in improving patient care, early detection, and targeted interventions.

The dataset utilized in this analysis comprises anonymized patient records from the local General Practice, encompassing a diverse range of demographic information, health measurements, disease diagnoses, and general health indicators. Rigorous data cleaning, preprocessing, and statistical techniques will be employed to ensure the reliability and validity of the results.

By conducting this statistical analysis on the local General Practice's patient data, we aim to contribute to the growing body of knowledge in healthcare research and provide evidence-based insights that can inform clinical practice, policy-making, and public health initiatives. The findings of this analysis have the potential to improve patient care, promote early intervention strategies, and contribute to the development of personalized healthcare approaches tailored to individual needs.

Bike Rental Analysis Using Linear Regression Modelling

This project aims to leverage the power of data analysis and statistical modeling to predict bike rentals over time, utilizing weather conditions as the main variable. A predictive model has been developed that can estimate future bike rentals based on weather conditions, enabling stakeholders to make data-driven decisions and take proactive measures to meet demand effectively. The project sheds light on the relationship between weather and bike rentals, providing valuable insights into the dynamics of bike-sharing systems and their response to environmental factors.
